MITIGATION PLAYBOOK
==================================================

Alternate Supplier Actions
--------------------------
1. Activate dual-sourcing for metallurgical coal and coke away from disrupted Queensland and Indian merchant coke exposure by requesting spot and term availability from U.S. Appalachia, Canada, Mozambique, Colombia, Indonesia, and captive coke producers; prioritize suppliers with confirmed rail-to-port capacity and blast-furnace-compatible coal/coke specifications.
2. Qualify substitute semi-finished and finished steel sources for China, Ukraine, and Europe exposure by shifting slab, billet, HRC, rebar, wire rod, and SBQ requirements to mills in Turkey, South Korea, Japan, Vietnam, Brazil, Mexico, the Middle East, and domestic producers where tariff and technical requirements allow.
3. Establish backup sourcing for alloy inputs affected by South African rail failures by pre-qualifying manganese ore, ferromanganese, silicomanganese, chrome ore, ferrochrome, and stainless/alloy steel input suppliers from Australia, Gabon, India, Kazakhstan, Turkey, Finland, Oman, and China, including chemistry, impurity, and delivery-lot validation.

Inventory Actions
-----------------
1. Increase safety stock targets for blast-furnace-critical inputs with near-term disruption exposure: coking coal, metallurgical coke, iron ore fines/lump/pellets, and coke blends; set minimum coverage thresholds by mill based on consumption rates, with priority given to facilities dependent on Australian coal, Brazilian ore, and Indian coke supply.
2. Ring-fence existing inventory of constrained semi-finished and finished steel products including slab, billet, HRC, plate, rebar, wire rod, SBQ, and steel pipe for firm customer orders, maintenance-critical production runs, and high-margin contracts; defer non-critical spot sales until replacement supply and inbound ETAs are confirmed.
3. Accelerate purchase timing for manganese, chrome, ferroalloys, coke, slab, billet, and European EAF-dependent long products before spot markets tighten further; use staged buys to avoid overexposure while locking near-term cover for the next 4 to 8 weeks of production.

Logistics Actions
-----------------
1. Reroute steelmaking inputs and semi-finished steel bound for Europe away from the Red Sea/Suez corridor where feasible by using Cape of Good Hope sailings with revised lead-time assumptions, Mediterranean transshipment alternatives, Atlantic Basin suppliers, and priority discharge windows at Rotterdam, Antwerp, Hamburg, Taranto, and Bilbao.
2. For Australian coking coal and Brazilian iron ore cargoes, confirm rail-to-port availability, vessel nomination status, laycan changes, and port queue lengths; prioritize vessels already loaded or at berth, and evaluate alternative export terminals or split shipments to reduce single-corridor dependence.
3. For Canadian port, South African rail, and Ukrainian export exposure, pre-book alternate freight capacity through U.S. West Coast or Gulf ports, Maputo/Walvis Bay/Durban alternatives where viable, Black Sea/Danube/rail corridors where available, and inland trucking or rail substitutions for urgent alloy, coal, slab, and coil movements.

Communication Actions
---------------------
1. Issue a procurement command-center notice covering the highest-risk cards: China emissions curbs, Queensland coal flooding, Ukrainian power disruption, South African manganese/chrome rail failures, Indian coke restrictions, European EAF curtailments, Red Sea diversions, and Brazilian ore delays; assign owners by commodity and region.
2. Contact impacted suppliers including BHP Mitsubishi Alliance, Glencore, Anglo American, Vale, CSN Mineração, Baowu, HBIS, Metinvest, ArcelorMittal Kryvyi Rih, South32, Assmang, Samancor, Tata Steel, JSW, and European EAF mills to obtain written updates on capacity, allocation, confirmed shipments, revised lead times, and force majeure risk.
3. Provide weekly leadership, operations, sales, and customer updates showing constrained materials, inventory coverage, expected production impacts, price exposure, mitigation status, and customer allocation decisions for steel products most exposed to shortages or longer lead times.
