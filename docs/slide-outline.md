I. Executive Summary & Industry Context
Slide 1: Proactive supply chain intelligence is essential to meet aggressive production targets and protect enterprise cash flow.
Transitioning supply chain management from reactive expediting to predictive optimization via an automated disruption radar agent.
Addressing structural blind spots in the deep supply chain to prevent cascading assembly line stoppages and protect Rate 47 deliverables.
Slide 2: Dynamic global volatility necessitates real-time risk monitoring to maintain accurate production schedules.
Traditional, static lead-time assumptions are increasingly failing against the reality of dynamic global supply chain friction.
Accurate production forecasting requires augmenting historical models with continuous, real-time monitoring of macroeconomic and localized public signals.
II. Current State Analysis & Risk Exposure
Slide 3: Comprehensive risk monitoring must extend beyond direct suppliers to capture deep-tier systemic disruptions.
Boeing maintains robust data on Tier-1 partners, yet a significant portion of critical, systemic disruptions originate in opaque Tier-2 through Tier-4 networks.
Continuously monitoring this extended supply base allows Boeing to detect localized shocks weeks before they physically cascade into the primary ecosystem.
Slide 4: Manual impact assessment across interconnected supply networks consumes critical response time.
Supply chain managers currently lack the decision support tools required to instantly trace how a sub-tier shock ripples through a complex Bill of Materials (BOM).
Without contextual mapping, identifying alternative mitigation paths becomes a manual, reactive exercise that squanders the "golden window" for intervention.
Slide 5: The operational cost of missing parts presents massive deferred revenue and margin degradation risks.
A missing $400 sub-tier bracket physically stalls the final assembly line, forcing costly out-of-sequence installations.
Out-of-sequence work exponentially compounds the risk of Foreign Object Debris (FOD), degrades unit margins, and ties up billions in Work-In-Progress inventory.
III. The Vision & Solution Architecture
Slide 6: Evolving supply chain risk monitoring into an AI-driven decision support system accelerates response to critical signals.
Transitioning from fragmented risk dashboards to an intelligent decision support tool that acts as a force multiplier for supply chain analysts.
Arming teams with data-driven mitigation playbooks, focusing the AI on high-value decision support rather than automated purchasing.
Slide 7: Synthesizing public signals establishes an early-warning radar for Boeing’s extended supply base.
Ingesting a structured taxonomy of public data (global news, labor union portals, weather alerts, and financial filings) to track known supplier health.
Continuously cross-referencing external Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) against Boeing’s established N-tier supplier database to spot anomalies instantly.
Slide 8: Knowledge graphs map the exact operational impact of distant global events on specific factory deliverables.
Translating macro-events into precise operational blast radii by applying GraphRAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) over Boeing's supply network.
Embedding deep business context so the AI understands the critical relationships between a delayed sub-tier component and the Renton or Charleston assembly floors.
Slide 9: Automated scoring models prioritize global threats based on likelihood, impact, and time-to-hit.
Utilizing the public signal taxonomy to calculate a composite risk score for every detected sub-tier anomaly.
Quantifying the exact Time-to-Hit (TTH) allows analysts to instantly separate immediate operational threats from long-term systemic noise.
Slide 10: Disruption Cards translate AI analysis and historical precedents into standardized, actionable intelligence.
The AI queries the knowledge graph to pull "lessons learned" and historical precedents from past supply chain shocks to formulate highly accurate mitigation strategies.
The live Agent Demo showcases 10-15 standard-format disruption cards detailing risk scores, specific impacted parts, and historically-grounded mitigation playbooks.
IV. Regulatory Compliance & Mitigation Strategy
Slide 11: Comprehensive playbooks dictate precise mitigation actions across inventory, logistics, and supplier communications.
Automatically drafting operational plays for alternate sourcing, premium logistics routing, and automated supplier status checks to sustain assembly line velocity.
Identifying and securing existing, certified inventory across parallel suppliers within the approved Boeing ecosystem via strategic buffer stocking.
Slide 12: Automating compliance workflows ensures all AI recommendations operate strictly within FAA safety regulations.
The FAA Type Certificate Reality: Unlike consumer manufacturing, aerospace components are strictly bound by "Type Certificates"—the legally inflexible recipe for an aircraft. The AI is hardcoded to never recommend unapproved material substitutions.
Exploiting Pre-Certified Networks: The AI is constrained to navigate within Boeing’s Approved Supplier List (ASL), securing secondary capacity only from alternate vendors who already hold regulatory clearance.
First Article Inspection (FAI) Acceleration: When shifting capacity to an alternate supplier, the system automatically queues the mandatory FAI documentation, ensuring regulatory compliance accelerates alongside production velocity.
V. Implementation, Risks, & Value Capture
Slide 13: A rigorous AI-based validation framework ensures high-fidelity data integrity and alert accuracy.
Multi-Agent Validation (AI Judges): Implementing a secondary "AI Judge" framework that critiques the primary agent's outputs against historical precedents to filter out hallucinations before they reach human analysts.
Human-in-the-Loop Oversight: Ensuring every high-impact mitigation playbook is vetted by senior supply chain subject matter experts before enterprise-wide execution.
Slide 14: Project Radar delivers measurable impact by protecting production stability and optimizing capital allocation.
Production Continuity & Margin Defense: Minimizing "travelled work" costs by ensuring part availability at the correct sequence point, eliminating out-of-sequence assembly.

Operational Efficiency: Buying back the "Golden Window" for intervention by converting weeks of manual sub-tier tracing into seconds of automated impact analysis.
